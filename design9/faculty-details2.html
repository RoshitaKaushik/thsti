About Dr. Bhattacharya

Dr. Jayanta Bhattacharya is a distinguished scientist and Dean at THSTI with over 25 years of research experience in translational immunology and infectious diseases. His pioneering work focuses on understanding host-pathogen interactions in the context of maternal and child health, particularly the immunological mechanisms governing mother-to-child transmission of HIV.

He leads a multidisciplinary research programme at the interface of basic science and clinical application, developing novel vaccine candidates and immunotherapeutic strategies for infectious diseases prevalent in low- and middle-income countries. His laboratory collaborates extensively with national and international institutions including NIH, WHO, and IAVI.

Research Overview

Ongoing Projects

Mucosal Immunity & HIV Transmission at the Maternal-Foetal Interface

Investigating innate and adaptive immune responses in the cervico-vaginal compartment to understand viral transmission barriers and identify novel vaccine targets.

DBT, Govt. of India 2022 – 2026 Active
Development of a Broadly Neutralising Antibody-Based HIV Vaccine

Collaborative programme with IAVI to engineer immunogens capable of eliciting broadly neutralising antibodies (bNAbs) against diverse HIV-1 clades circulating in South Asia.

IAVI / NIH 2021 – 2025 Active
Neonatal Innate Immune Programming & Susceptibility to Infections

Studying epigenetic and transcriptomic signatures in cord blood immune cells to identify early life factors that modulate susceptibility to neonatal sepsis and respiratory infections.

Wellcome Trust / DBT 2020 – 2024 Completed
